## AN UPDATE ON GOOD CHOLESTEROL
Lipids are fatty substances that are essential component of the human body. They are important for generating and storing energy, forming hormones and vitamin D, building cell membranes and providing insulation to nerve conduction and prevent heat loss.
Lipids are not water soluble, and therefore they cannot travel in the bloodstream easily. Therefore, lipids are transported in the bloodstream through water soluble capsules called Lipoprotiens. These Lipoproreins travel easily in the circulation. If you are trying to avoid heart disease, you should keep tabs on your blood cholesterol values, especially your low density 
lipoprotein or LDL ( bad cholesterol). Too much LDL in the bloodstream can create artery clogging plaques, increase your risk of heart attack and stroke. 

Your cholesterol report also called lipid profile or lipid panel includes high density lipoprotein or HDL. HDL, often referred to as good cholesterol since people with high HDL levels are less likely to develop cardiovascular disease.
HDL level less than 40 for men and less than 50 for women is considered low. However higher the level of HDL, better it is . For men more than 50 and for women more than 60 is considered a good value.
Higher HDL levels are often linked with behavior like eating, healthy diet, getting regular exercise and not smoking.

BENEFITS OF HDL
Unlike other Lipoproteins, HDL is anti-atherogenic( prevents clogging of the arteries), it helps to remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream and arterial walls (Reverse cholesterol transport). It also has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant and even antithrombotic activity.( meaning preventing clot formation). 
It’s  often referred to as a garbage truck, because it collects extra cholesterol or garbage from the bloodstream and arteries, and then transports it to the liver where it is recycled or disposed. On that basis, there were large clinical trials that tested about five drugs known as CEPT INHIBITORS in patients with low HDL levels . These drugs reliably raise the HDL level, as much as 60 to 100%. However they failed to reduce the risk of heart attack or other cardiovascular events. It was thought that the number of garbage trucks do not matter, but how good they were picking up trash from the circulation matters the most. That may be because  these drugs increase the wrong kind of HDL cholesterol. 

Several studies have shown a clear and indisputable role for HDL cholesterol as an independent risk factor for cardiovascular diseases. Lower  the HDL cholesterol level, the higher the risk for myocardial infarction, strokes and other cardiovascular diseases. However, increasing the HDL cholesterol level does not reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease and adverse events. This may be because HDL comes in different shapes and sizes. Some types are spherical while others are donut shaped. Some types of HDL are great at picking up excess cholesterol from the circulation  and from artery walls, a process called reverse cholesterol transport, while other types are not. Scientist also think that HDL particles do not appear to work properly in an inflammatory environment, such as atherosclerotic vessel walls. 
Anti-inflammatory actions of HDL are impaired in atherosclerosis. 

It seems that quality is more important than quantity when it comes to HDL in cardiovascular disease. Therefore assessing HDL function might be more important than determining the HDL cholesterol levels. However, test that measure HDL function are not widely available. Once we are able to more consistently evaluate HDL function , HDL may become a better target for improving cardiovascular disease risk. As of now, studies have shown that targeting HDL with medications may not be helpful in preventing heart disease. We need a better empty garbage truck with a better trash compactor.

However, these findings should not distract people from focusing on the factors you can control, namely by making sure that LDL cholesterol is below 100 mg/dL or even lower if you have risk factors or already have cardiovascular disease. This is true, even if you have a high HDL level, which shouldn’t be considered overly reassuring or a reason to ignore elevated LDL cholesterol. Even though, at present medications may not help, lifestyle changes like regular exercise, weight reduction, and not smoking will all helpful in preventing Cardiovascular disease.
